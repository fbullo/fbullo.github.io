Overview (November 2011) prepared by Luca Carlone

HOUSING

Renting a room in Santa Barbara is quite expensive and prices vary depending on the area. Downtown Santa Barbara has plenty of shops, pubs and bars, and it is probably the most expensive area. There is a bus connecting downtown with USCB in about 20 minutes (10 miles). Isla Vista, instead, is an area right close to the university and the majority of its residents are university students (19-22 years-old college students). This area may result a bit chaotic but it is very nice for young students. There are several student residences, in which it is possible to find single and shared rooms at good prices. Usually the rent varies between 600 and 800 USD, plus a consistent deposit that is required when starting the contract. Finally, there is Goleta, located between Isla Vista and Downtown. This can be a good compromise: from there it is possible to reach UCSB by bike in 20 min and the rent is usually smaller than downtown. Renting a single (decent) room in Goleta would cost between 650 and 900 USD. The following links can help in finding an accommodation:
http://motion.me.ucsb.edu/visitors/
http://santabarbara.craigslist.org/roo/

(*) A solution suggested by a former visiting scholar can be found at this link (availability depends on the dates of the visit) It may be a good idea to select some possible accommodations before arriving in Santa Barbara, possibly arranging appointments for taking a look at the candidate accommodations. Then, one could stay in hotel for some days (7-10 days) while choosing the best solution.

PUBLIC TRANSPORTATION

Buses are punctual but the lines do not cover completely the town, so one has to pondered if to use buses (or bicycle plus buses) depending on the area of the town one is living in. Cheap cars can be found on Craigslist(http://santabarbara.craigslist.org/), but for short stays the cheapest and the easiest solution is to reach UCSB by bicycle from Goleta or Isla Vista. It is possible to buy a decent bike for 80-100 USD.

BANK ACCOUNT

It is easy and inexpensive to open an American bank account. This can be useful for enabling payments from the university and for avoiding to have troubles with the withdrawal limits of the international accounts. These limits can be particularly unlikely in the first month of the stay (for long stays), when one has to sustain several expenses (deposit, rent, transportation, etc.).
