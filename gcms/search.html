Keywords
Case sensitive? (on means "true")

Here are some tips. See the paragraph following for an explanation of why these things are as they are.

  1. To search for adjacent words (e.g., ``Riemannian manifold''), separate words with "\+" (e.g. ``Riemannian\+manifold'').
  2. To search for occurrences of multiple strings, enter a comma/space separated list of keywords.
  3. To get an exact word match, add "\+\" to the beginning and end of the string (e.g., ``\+Riemann\+'' will find occurrences of the word ``Riemann'').
  4. To match the beginning of a word, add "\+" to the beginning of the search string, and add "\w*\+" to the end (e.g., ``\+Riemann\w*\+'' will find all occurrences of words beginning with ``Riemann''). Similar strategies will get matches at the end of words - the "\w*" is a wildcard.

The files being searched are not the full LaTeX files for the book. Instead, these files are processed using the program delatex (among other things) to eliminate most of the LaTeX commands. The words in the file are then arranged into a list with a "+" separating words. There is no punctuation in the list. If you know Perl, then the "+" separator will be helpful. Note that delatex does not always do a perfect job of removing LaTeX commands. This is particularly true for our book, since we use many LaTeX macros. Therefore, you may get some spurious search results.
