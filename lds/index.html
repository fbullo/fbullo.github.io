Content

  • Chapter 1 “Dynamical Systems: Definition and Scientific Examples” Introduces the concept of dynamical systems with examples, including one-dimensional growth models, periodic solutions, and visualization techniques.

  • Chapter 2 “Mechanical and Electromechanical Systems” Covers modeling of mechanical systems (single and multi-degree), rotational systems, electrical circuits, and electromechanical devices like DC motors.

  • Chapter 3 “Thermal and Fluid Systems” Discusses heat transfer, fluid dynamics, and linearization of nonlinear systems, with applications to thermal and fluid engineering problems.

  • Chapter 4 “The Laplace Transform” Introduces the Laplace transform and its inverse, applying them to solve linear differential equations and discussing key properties and transform pairs.

  • Chapter 5 “The Transfer Function and Time Responses of Dynamical Systems” Explains transfer functions, impulse and step responses for first-, second-, and higher-order systems, including stability tests and special cases.

  • Chapter 6 “Frequency Response, Resonances, and Beats in Dynamical Systems” Analyzes frequency response, resonance, lightly damped systems, and beating phenomena, including filter concepts and sinusoidal steady-state analysis.

  • Chapter 7 “Control Systems: Basic Definitions and Concepts” Introduces control systems, comparing open- and closed-loop control, with static and dynamic models, and highlighting advantages of feedback.

  • Chapter 8 “Control Systems Design” Covers feedback diagrams, design strategies for low-order systems, stability issues, and block diagrams for multi-input multi-output control systems.
